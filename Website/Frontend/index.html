Introduction

Welcome to the London Housing Market Explorer — your interactive guide to understanding how London’s housing market has evolved across all 33 boroughs.

Whether you’re a resident, a student, or simply curious about London’s neighborhoods, this platform lets you explore how prices, livability, and community dynamics change across the city. Hover, compare, and discover what makes each borough unique — from the fast-changing East End to the leafy suburbs of the South and West.

Built by students of the MSc Connected Environments at University College London, this project turns complex datasets from the ONS, GLA, and HM Land Registry into clear visual stories you can interact with — not just read about.

Our aim is simple: to help you make sense of the numbers behind London’s housing market and see the city through data — dynamic, diverse, and constantly changing.

About

London Housing Market Explorer is an interactive data visualisation platform developed by MSc Connected Environments students at University College London (UCL). The project transforms complex housing data into an accessible, engaging interface that allows users to explore long-term trends and spatial patterns across London’s 33 boroughs.

Using open datasets from HM Land Registry, Office for National Statistics (ONS), Transport for London (TfL), and the Greater London Authority (GLA), the website visualises how property prices, affordability, and neighbourhood characteristics have evolved between 1995 and 2025. Users can compare boroughs, track trends, and discover their ideal neighbourhood through interactive modules combining map-based exploration, time-series charts, and dynamic ranking visualisations.

The platform also introduces a Find Your Ideal Neighbourhood tool, where visitors can adjust real-time sliders for factors such as commute time, environmental quality, and access to amenities. Each adjustment instantly recalculates borough suitability scores, creating a data-driven way to understand London’s livability from multiple perspectives.

Developed using Node.js, Express, and D3.js, the project integrates front-end interactivity with a back-end API and caching system for fast, responsive visualisation.
